# PRD: Family Recipes Website

## 1. Summary

Create a clean, static website to collect and preserve family recipes. The MVP will prioritize browsing and discovery across meal-type categories with search and filters, plus a simple email-capture UI for future engagement.

## 2. Problem Statement

Family recipes are scattered (paper and various sources) and risk being lost. Existing recipe platforms are cluttered with ads and non-essential features. The client wants a single, aesthetic place that presents recipes clearly and preserves them for family use.

## 3. Goals (Launch)

- Primary: Enable easy browsing and discovery of recipes by category and search.
- Secondary: Capture emails (visual-only capture at launch) for future newsletters and personalized recommendations.

## 4. Primary Persona

Nely — stay-at-home parent, loves to bake, keeps a recipe book. Comfortable with basic websites; uses phone and iPad. Goal: access all recipes in one clean, readable location.

## 5. Success Metrics

- Number of recipes uploaded (coverage)
- Number of categories/tabs created (organization)
- Completeness of each recipe (ingredients, steps, images)

## 6. MVP Features

- Static recipe listing pages organized by categories/tabs (breakfast, lunch, dinner, dessert)
- Client-side search (keyword and ingredient search)
- Client-side filters (dietary preferences, meal type)
- Responsive design for phone and iPad
- Simple email capture UI (no backend integration at launch)
- Recipe metadata: title, ingredients, steps, tools, tags
- Placeholder/fallback images for missing originals

## 7. Content & Assets

- Provided: recipe text (ingredients, steps, tools), contact email.
- Needed: final photos — fallback to general internet images allowed; verify licensing/attribution for externally sourced assets.

## 8. Constraints & Timeline

- Static site at launch (no integrations required).
- Desired launch: this Friday.
- Budget: to be confirmed; prioritize free/static hosting (GitHub Pages, Netlify).

## 9. Integrations (Deferred)

- Email provider, analytics, and social sharing widgets are optional post-launch additions. The site will ship static first; integrations added later when ready.

## 10. Risks & Mitigations

- Missing images: use placeholders and mark recipes needing original photos.
- Content backlog: publish a prioritized subset of recipes for Friday, schedule remaining uploads after launch.
- Incomplete recipe details: require client review/approval before publishing; flag incomplete recipes.
- Licensing: review and attribute or replace any externally sourced recipes/images that require permission.

## 11. Next Steps

1. Confirm the subset of recipes to publish for Friday (priority list).
2. Choose hosting for the static site (recommended: GitHub Pages or Netlify).
3. Implement MVP pages, search, filters, responsive styling, and email-capture UI.
4. QA content completeness against success metrics.
5. Post-launch: add analytics and email provider integration; replace placeholder images with originals.
