## Client Brief

**Client:** 
Nely Molendyk, my mom

**Purpose:** 
The purpose is to organize her recipes and have them all in one place. This solves disorganization and the prevention of family recipes being lost by having everything online in one place.

**Audience:** 
The audience includes family members, recipe explorers, stay-at-home moms, chefs, moms/friends. The recipes are for different types of meals and to give inspiration to others to ease their pain point of not knowing what to cook each day.


**Key action:**
The most important thing that a visitor should do is sign up for a subscription, so they will continue to come back to the page and receive updates with new recipes. Success looks like the number of subscriptions increasing each month as well as the number of shares/likes of the recipes increasing each month. Right now, the focus of the website is to provide accessible recipes to others and organize them in one place, when I further the website to include dynamic features in the future, the subcriptions would then be the key action.


**Pages/sections needed:**
- Types of meals (split into sections like breakfast, lunch, dinner, dessert)
- Dietary preferences
- Home page

**Content status:** 
I have the list of ingredients, steps, tools needed as well as her email address. I still need images of the final product (although I may have to look up images online because she doesn't have images of the final product) and her book with all of the recipes in it.


**Style preferences:** 
She wants to have a clean looking website and the colors include light pink and brown. She also wants the page to be simplistic with the layout and images and have a fancy but flowy font for the text.

**Inspiration sites:**
- [[Site 1](https://food52.com/story/5924-introducing-recipe-collections)] - Simple, easy to understand, organized tabs, not much color, and the font isn't aesthetic.
- [[Site 2](https://fabrx.co/preview/tastebite/)] - Like the font, pictures, pretty, bland, favorite out of the three, likes the pink color, nice to look at.
- [[Site 3](https://cooking.nytimes.com/)] - Doesn't like how there are big ads, doesn't like the side bar of articles, easy to understand/read, not much color/font, not the most appealing.


## Layout Plan

- [[Reference site](https://fabrx.co/preview/tastebite/)]
- Keep the pink color and simplistic style.
- Keep pictures but add my own that still maintain the "clean and simplistic" vibe.
- Make the background light pink because this site is too bland, needs more color.
- Change the font to one less sharp, has more flow and maybe to a brown color.
- Maybe add borders around the pictures to help with the blandness.
- Include boxes around ingredients and bold specific words in steps to help with the aesthetic view.
